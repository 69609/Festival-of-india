Celebrate India

Discover the vibrant tapestry of Indian festivals that bring communities together in joy, tradition, and cultural celebration

Major Festivals

Diwali

The Festival of Lights celebrating the victory of light over darkness, good over evil. Families light diyas and burst crackers.

October/November

Holi

The Festival of Colors marking the arrival of spring. People play with colors, dance, and celebrate together.

March

Navratri

Nine nights dedicated to Goddess Durga with traditional dances, fasting, and vibrant celebrations across India.

September/October

Durga Puja

Bengal's biggest festival honoring Goddess Durga with elaborate pandals, cultural programs, and community feasts.

September/October

Eid

Celebrated by Muslims across India with prayers, feasts, and sharing of gifts marking the end of Ramadan.

Varies

Onam

Kerala's harvest festival with elaborate flower carpets, traditional dances, and the famous Onam Sadhya feast.

August/September

Regional Celebrations

North India

Karva Chauth, Dussehra, Lohri, and Baisakhi showcase the rich cultural heritage of northern states with wheat harvest celebrations and devotional fervor.

South India

Pongal, Ugadi, Vishu, and Onam celebrate rice harvests, new years, and regional deities with traditional music, dance, and elaborate feasts.

East India

Durga Puja, Kali Puja, and Poila Boishakh highlight Bengali culture with artistic pandals, cultural performances, and literary celebrations.

West India

Ganesh Chaturthi, Gudi Padwa, and Navratri bring communities together with street processions, folk dances, and vibrant decorations.

Festival FAQs

When is the best time to visit India for festivals?

+
October to March is ideal as it covers Diwali, Durga Puja, Christmas, and many regional festivals with pleasant weather across most of India.
